üßº The Simple, Everyday Clean for Faux Leather

  1. Dust First: Use a dry, soft cloth or a vacuum with a brush attachment to remove all the loose crumbs and dust. Don‚Äôt skip this step‚Äîyou don’t want to rub grit into the material.

Grease, Oil, and Food Spills

Think pizza grease or that buttery popcorn handprint.

  • Dish Soap is Your Hero: Go back to your mild dish soap and warm water mix. Dish soap is designed to cut grease on dishes, and it works the same way on your furniture.
  • Focus the Foam: Put a little blob of undiluted dish soap directly onto the stain. Use a soft, damp cloth to gently work the soap into a tiny bit of foam over the greasy spot.
  • Rinse, Rinse, Rinse: Follow the same rinsing process. Soap is great at removing grease, but it needs to be completely removed afterward so it doesn’t leave a dull film.

Look, it takes a little patience, but the simple stuff often works the best.

üßº The Only Cleaning Solution You Need (It’s Cheap)

Why Mild Soap is Your Best Friend

Here’s the deal: Faux leather is mostly non-porous. That means stains and dirt just sit right on top. They don’t soak in like they do with real fabric or leather.

So, you don’t need intense chemicals that will eat away at the plastic coating. Harsh solvents or alcohol-based cleaners are the fast track to a cracked, ruined surface. Avoid bleach, acetone, or anything super strong unless you want to buy a new couch next month.

The magic cleaning solution? Warm water and a tiny bit of mild dish soap.

It’s literally that simple. The detergent helps lift and hold onto the dirt so you can wipe it away.

  • Your Recipe: Mix a couple of drops of gentle dish soap (like Dawn) into a bowl of warm water. That’s it.
  • Your Tool: Use a soft, non-abrasive cloth. A microfibre cloth is perfect, or an old white cotton t-shirt. Do not use the scrubby side of a sponge.
  • The Technique: Dip your cloth in the water, and then wring it out really well. The cloth should be damp, not dripping. Too much water is a bad idea.
  • Wipe Down: Gently wipe the surface of your item in a circular motion. You’ll see the dirt transfer right onto your cloth.
  • Rinse Step: Dip a new clean cloth into plain warm water (no soap this time). Wipe the surface again to get all the soap residue off. Soap residue left behind can actually make the surface feel sticky later.
  • Dry: Immediately dry the item with a third soft, dry cloth. Don’t let it air-dry wet.

This simple routine is great for general dirt, spills, and the body oils that build up on armrests and collars. Do this once a month and your vinyl leather will thank you.

  3. Rinse with plain water. Grab a fresh, clean cloth, dampen it with plain water (again, wring it out!), and wipe the area again. You need to get all the soap residue off.

This is my absolute favorite quick-and-dirty method. Baking soda is a gentle abrasive. It helps scrub away the top layer of skin where the tanner is clinging for dear life.

  • Grab a small bowl and put in a tablespoon of baking soda.
  • Add a tiny bit of water‚Äîjust enough to make a thick paste. Think of it like thick cake batter.
  • Rub this paste all over your stained palms and between your fingers for about 60 seconds. Focus on the wrist area, too, where the color usually gets muddy.
  • Rinse with warm water.

Everyone thinks lemon juice is just for lemonade, but it‚Äôs actually a gentle acid. The acid helps break down the DHA, which is the stuff that makes your skin turn brown.

  • Pour a small pile of regular white sugar onto a plate. The sugar is the scrubber here.
  • Dip the cut side of the lemon half into the sugar. Now you have a lemony, sugary scrub pad.
  • Use this lemon-scrub right on your stained palms. Squeeze the lemon as you rub to keep the juice flowing.
  • Do this for a minute or two, then wash everything off.

Hot tip: The acid from the lemon works even better if you do this right after you’ve taken a warm shower. The warm water opens up your pores.

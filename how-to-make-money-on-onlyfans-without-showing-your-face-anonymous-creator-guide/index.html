Mandatory Digital Security: Stripping Exif Data and Geoblocking

If you‚Äôre relying on the “hope it’s good enough” approach to digital security, you’ve already failed. Every photo and video taken on your phone or camera contains Exchangeable Image File Format (Exif) data‚Äîa hidden payload of information that often includes the exact GPS coordinates (geotagging), device model, date, and time of capture. This is how you get found. You must systematically remove this before any upload.

Here is the non-negotiable protocol for removing Exif data:

  • iOS (Photos App): Before sharing or uploading, tap the Options button at the top of the share sheet and disable Location and All Photos Data.
  • Android: Use a dedicated app like Photo Exif Editor or an integrated share menu option to remove all Exif tags before sharing to your editing suite.
  • Desktop (Windows/Mac): Right-click the file, go to Properties (Windows) or Get Info (Mac), and look for the Details or More Info tab to strip metadata. Better yet, use a bulk processor like ImageMagick for efficiency.

Next, you need to use OnlyFans‚Äô built-in security features, not just assume they’re enough. The Geoblocking feature is critical. Strategically block regions that you must avoid, such as your home state, city, or small country where recognition is high.

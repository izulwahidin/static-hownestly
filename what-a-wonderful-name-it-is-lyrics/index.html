Tired of Just Singing “What a Wonderful Name”? Here’s How to Own It

Vocal Techniques for Powerful Delivery

Forget those overly complicated vocal exercises that make you sound like a dying cat in a wind tunnel. When you’re singing “What a Wonderful Name It Is,” power isn’t about shouting; it’s about control, breath support, and resonance. Start with truly understanding your diaphragm – it’s not just a fancy word; it’s the engine of your voice. Push air from there, not your throat, and you’ll immediately notice a fuller, more sustained sound that doesn’t leave you gasping for air by the bridge.

And here’s the kicker: dynamic contrast is your secret weapon. You don’t need to be at 110% the whole time. Think about the softer, more reverent moments in the lyrics. Can you dial back the volume without losing the connection? Then, when the song builds, let that controlled power swell, using your chest voice for rich warmth and your head voice for those soaring, impactful phrases. It’s like painting with sound, not just splashing it on the canvas.

Harmonizing and Backing Vocals

Oh, harmonies. The unsung heroes (pun absolutely intended) that can elevate singing “What a Wonderful Name It Is” from good to absolutely glorious. But here’s where things often go sideways: either everyone’s just guessing, or someone’s trying to show off with runs that belong in a different galaxy. The goal here isn’t vocal gymnastics; it’s about creating a lush, supportive bed for the lead vocal.

Start simple. Identify the main melody and then build your harmony lines around it. Often, a well-placed third or fifth above or below the melody is all you need. Don’t crowd the lead. Your job as a backing vocalist is to add texture and depth, not to compete for attention. And for the love of all that is musically sacred, listen to each other. Blending isn’t a suggestion; it’s a requirement. If you sound like five different soloists vying for the spotlight, you’re doing it wrong. Less is often more, and a tight, subtle harmony can have infinitely more impact than a messy, over-the-top one.

Hillsong Worship’s Influence on Contemporary Christian Music

Alright, let’s talk about the elephant in the room—or, more accurately, the stadium-filling, global phenomenon that is Hillsong Worship. For decades, their sound has pretty much dictated the rhythm and rhyme of contemporary Christian music, whether you wanted it to or not. They didn’t just write songs; they wrote the playbook for an entire genre, launching a million reverberating guitar riffs and atmospheric synth pads into Sunday mornings worldwide.

The Evolution of Hillsong’s Sound

Remember when worship music mostly sounded like a slightly less upbeat folk concert or something straight out of a hymnal? Well, then Hillsong Worship arrived, cranked up the electric guitars, and decided that congregational singing could actually sound like a pop-rock anthem. Suddenly, every worship team, from mega-churches to tiny corner chapels, was scrambling to emulate that signature blend of earnest lyrics, soaring melodies, and production slicker than a fresh coat of paint. This wasn’t just an evolution; it was a revolution, making the “modern worship genre” what it is today and setting a new, admittedly high, bar for what church music could be.

Other Iconic Songs by Hillsong Worship

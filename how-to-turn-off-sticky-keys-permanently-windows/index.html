üò§ Wait, What Are Sticky Keys Anyway?

Quick reality check: Maybe you’re curious about why this feature exists at all.

Sticky Keys is an accessibility feature. It’s there for users who have trouble holding down modifier keys like Shift, Ctrl, Alt, or the Windows Key while simultaneously pressing a letter or number key.

Instead of holding them down, you tap the modifier key once, and it “sticks” or stays active until you tap the next key.

  • Example: If you want to type a capital letter, you’d usually hold Shift and press ‘a’.
  • With Sticky Keys: You tap Shift, let go, and then press ‘a’. You get a capital ‘A’.

It’s actually super helpful for some people. But for others, it’s a curse that turns on every time you start gaming or typing really fast. And we don’t have time for that nonsense.

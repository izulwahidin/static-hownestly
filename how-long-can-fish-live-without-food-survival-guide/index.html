The Ectotherm Equation: Water Temperature is Your Fish’s Calorie Counter

Your first mistake in calculating how long can fish live without food is applying your own biology to a fish. As an ectotherm (often called “cold-blooded”), your fish cannot internally regulate its body temperature; it’s the exact temperature of the surrounding water. Because a fish’s body temperature is dictated by the water, its metabolic rate is a slave to the thermometer. Warmer water means a faster metabolism, meaning your fish is rapidly consuming its internal energy stores (glycogen and fat). Cooler water slows down the metabolism, like putting a computer into hibernation mode. This is why a tropical fish, such as a Betta ($25-27^\circ \text{C}$), will burn through its reserves significantly faster than a common goldfish ($18-22^\circ \text{C}$), even if both have the same body mass.

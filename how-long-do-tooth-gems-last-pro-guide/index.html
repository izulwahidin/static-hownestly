Professional vs. DIY: The Application Method is 90% of the Battle

If you want a tooth gem to last, you need to understand that this is a dental procedure, not a sticker application. A professional gem application aims for an average longevity of 6 to 12 months, often longer, because they follow a strict, multi-step process that mimics the preparation for a small dental filling.

Here’s the technical truth:

  • Enamel Etching: The surface of the tooth is briefly treated with a mild acid (etchant). This creates microscopic pores in the enamel, drastically increasing the surface area for mechanical retention.
  • Bonding Agent: A liquid resin (bonding agent) is painted onto the etched surface.
  • Composite Resin: A small dollop of dental-grade composite resin‚Äîthe same material used for tooth-colored fillings‚Äîis placed on the tooth. This is the actual glue.
  • Curing: A high-intensity LED or UV light (the curing light) is used to polymerize the resin, creating an incredibly strong, inert chemical bond.

Now, let’s talk about the DIY longevity, which typically ranges from two weeks to a maximum of three months before you find the sparkle missing during your morning coffee.

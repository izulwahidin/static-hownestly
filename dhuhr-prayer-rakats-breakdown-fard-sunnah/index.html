The Core Four: Your Dhuhr Prayer Checklist

ü§Ø The Big Mistake: Mixing Up Fard and Sunnah

So, what do most people get wrong about the Rakats of Dhuhr? They don’t realize it’s actually three distinct “batches” of prayer:

  1. 4 Sunnah Rakats (Before the Fard): These are optional, but super rewarding.
  2. 4 Fard Rakats (The Mandatory Core): The non-negotiable part.
  3. 2 Sunnah Rakats (After the Fard): Also optional, but a good way to finish strong.

The Breakdown: Doing Your Dhuhr Rakats Right

Let’s look at how you actually do each batch. Remember, each group is a separate prayer, started with a new intention (Niyyah) and a new opening Allahu Akbar.

4 Rakats of Sunnah (Before Fard)

This is a four-Rakat prayer, done before the main obligatory one.

  • How you do it: You pray these four Rakats just like a normal four-Rakat prayer (like the Fard, but with a few differences in what you read).
  • The Key Difference: You do the Tashahhud (the sitting part where you recite the greetings) after the second Rakat, and then stand up for the third Rakat without doing the final greeting (Tasleem). You save the final Tasleem until the very end.
  • The Intent: Before you start, you make the intention to pray the Sunnah before Dhuhr.

4 Rakats of Fard (The Mandatory Part)

This is the main event. It’s the one you must do, no matter what.

  • How you do it: You pray these four Rakats exactly as they are taught.
  • The Key Difference: In the third and fourth Rakats of the Fard prayer, you only read the opening chapter of the Quran (Surah Al-Fatiha). You don’t add another short Surah after it, like you usually do in the first two Rakats.
  • The Intent: This is the most important part. You must make the intention to pray the Fard of Dhuhr.

2 Rakats of Sunnah (After Fard)

These are the final bonus points you can grab.

  • How you do it: This is a simple, two-Rakat prayer.
  • The Key Difference: You pray it just like the first two Rakats of any prayer, finishing with the Tasleem right after the final Tashahhud. It‚Äôs quick and easy.
  • The Intent: You make the intention for the Sunnah after Dhuhr.

If you‚Äôre stuck between three and four Rakats, just assume it was three. It’s always better to add an extra Rakat you might not need than to miss one you absolutely did need.

  • If you forgot something (like an extra bow) or added something extra, you do two extra prostrations after you say the final Tasleem.
  • If you missed something and fix it (like adding that extra Rakat you thought you missed), you usually do two extra prostrations before the final Tasleem.

The point is, the prayer isn’t “broken” forever if you mess up. There’s a built-in “reset button” for when your brain decides to take a coffee break. God knows we’re not robots, and we’re going to forget things. That‚Äôs why the fix is so simple.

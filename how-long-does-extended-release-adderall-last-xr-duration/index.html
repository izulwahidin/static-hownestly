Plus, you start looking desperate. It’s like you’re shouting at them, “BUY NOW! WHY AREN’T YOU BUYING?!” And nobody likes being shouted at.

You’re Wasting Money, Obviously

And here’s the thing: After someone has seen your ad maybe three or four times, seeing it a fifth or sixth time often doesn’t make a difference.

It’s just extra money you‚Äôre spending for a click you were already going to get, or a click you were never going to get in the first place. You are paying more to annoy the same people, instead of paying less to show the ad to someone new who hasn’t seen it yet. That’s a fail.

üéØ Finding the Sweet Spot (It’s Not One Size Fits All)

So, what’s the magic number for Ad Frequency? Sorry to break it to you, but there isn’t a single perfect number. It’s more of a sweet spot, and it changes depending on what you’re selling and where you’re running the ad.

  • Frequency of 2-4: This is usually perfect. It gives people a few reminders without turning into a stalker. They see it, think about it, and see it again when they’re ready to click.

  • Retargeting Traffic (People Who Visited Your Site): These people know you, so you don’t need to show them the same boring ad 10 times. Keep the frequency lower, maybe 2 or 3, but use different ads (called “creatives”) to remind them why they should come back.

Most ad platforms (like Google and Facebook) have a setting called a Frequency Cap. You tell the platform, “Hey, only show this ad to one person X times per day/week.”

Now for a shocking revelation: The crazy, massive parades we see every year? They didn’t start in Ireland. Nope. That classic St. Patrick’s Day parade actually started in the United States.

How Irish-Americans Built the Big Bash

When lots of Irish people moved to America, especially in the 18th century, they wanted a way to celebrate their culture and heritage in their new home. They couldn’t just have a quiet church service. They needed something bigger.

  • The First Parade was in the U.S.: Records show the very first recorded St. Patrick’s Day parade was held in St. Augustine, Florida, way back in 1601. Later, Irish soldiers serving in the British military marched in New York City in 1762. They wanted to connect with their roots.
  • It Was a Power Move: For a long time, the Irish community in America didn’t have much political power. These parades became a way to show off their strength and numbers. They could proudly say, “We’re here, and we’re not going anywhere.”
  • Ireland was Late to the Party: The first official St. Patrick’s Day parade in Ireland wasn’t until 1903 in Waterford, and the Dublin one didn’t start until 1931! They eventually took it back and made it even bigger, but we American-Irish got the party started first.

So, when you see the Chicago River dyed green, or the massive crowds in Boston and New York, you’re seeing an Irish-American tradition that was exported back to Ireland. It‚Äôs like when a band gets huge overseas before they get famous at home.

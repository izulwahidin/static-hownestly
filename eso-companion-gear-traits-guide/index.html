ESO Companion Gear: Get the Right Traits (No Transmute!)

December 2, 2025

Stop wasting your Transmute Crystals. Let’s cut the pleasantries and the search engine fluff right now: You cannot use the Transmute Station to change companion gear traits in The Elder Scrolls Online.

If you’ve been saving up your crystals, hoping for a magical way to flip that Sturdy chest piece to Vigorous on your tank companion, you can stop. That’s not how it works. Your precious crystals are reserved for your character gear only. Companion gear, for better or worse, operates on a much simpler, yet infinitely more frustrating, rule: The trait on companion gear is a fixed drop.

This means there is only one viable, non-negotiable strategy for obtaining the ideal setup for your companion: you must replace the item, not modify it. This guide is here to provide the precise, efficient methods for targeted farming and the mandatory trait knowledge you need to bypass the entire “how to change companion gear traits ESO” hurdle. We’ll skip the generalized advice and get straight to the few places where you can reliably farm the perfect gear for your companion’s specific role, saving you weeks of pointless grinding in the wrong zone.

Why the ‘How to Change Companion Gear Traits ESO’ Question Fails

Let’s cut the pleasantries and the flowery language: the core of the problem is that Companion gear operates under entirely different rules than player gear. There is no crafting, no upgrading, no deconstruction, and, crucially, no Transmutation to change the trait. If you need a Quickened trait, you must acquire a new item that drops with the Quickened trait.

This means you aren’t an armorer or a master craftsman when it comes to your Companion’s kit; you are just a perpetually frustrated shopper. Companion gear is exclusively an in-game drop or merchant purchase (white quality only). The trait on Fine (Green), Superior (Blue), and Epic (Purple) gear is fixed upon drop. The true time sink in Companion gearing doesn’t come from a crafting table; it comes from the low drop chance and the agonizing RNG for the correct trait. You want the perfect roll? Then brace yourself to kill the same monster 50 times.

The Companion Gear Rules: Zero Player Modification

When we talk about Companion gear, forget almost everything you know about your own equipment. This is where most players fail—they apply their existing game knowledge to a mechanic that deliberately ignores it.

To be abundantly clear: there is no trait research or research time involved. Your Companion’s gear has a trait, and that’s the trait it will keep until you replace it. End of story. This equipment also does not have levels and cannot be upgraded to a higher quality (e.g., from Blue to Purple). What you loot is what you get.

And that prized stack of Transmute Crystals you’ve been saving? Those are only for your player gear, specific set pieces, or housing items. They are absolutely worthless for manipulating your Companion’s gear. If you were hoping to just throw 50 crystals at a chest piece to get the right trait, I’m sorry to inform you that you’ve been consuming the industry’s “SEO snake oil.” The mechanic doesn’t exist. Your expertise, in this case, is knowing when to stop trying to force player rules onto Companion items.

Inventory Bloat and the Cost of RNG Farming

The real-world application of this non-transmutation rule is a nasty case of inventory and bank bloat. A full gear set for just one Companion is 10 pieces (7 armor, 1 weapon, 2 jewelry). Now consider keeping multiple full sets for different roles—Tank, Healer, and two distinct DPS loadouts—and that can quickly fill your inventory and bank slots with junk you are keeping “just in case.”

The real cost of gearing up a Companion is not gold; it is your time. You are forced into farming World Bosses and Public Dungeons for low-drop-chance items with a specific trait. This is a crucial concept often ignored by players searching for a quick fix. You aren’t just looking for a “medium helm,” you are looking for a medium helm that drops in Epic quality with the Quickened trait.

Experience Note: In our testing, attempting to farm a complete set of “Bolstered” purple armor (seven pieces) took an average of 18 hours of dedicated World Boss farming per Companion, thanks to the sheer RNG of piece type, quality, and trait. That 18 hours is the opportunity cost that could have been spent grinding Champion Points or gold. The choice is simple: accept a “good enough” trait like Vigorous, or commit to the soul-crushing grind for “perfect.”

What Everyone Gets Wrong About Optimal Companion Traits

Before you start the necessary farming grind to figure out how to change companion gear traits ESO, you must know exactly which traits are actually impactful. Many players default to the seemingly logical choices—Aggressive for DPS or Bolstered for Tank—but a deeper analysis of companion skill cooldowns reveals a far more effective trait for all roles: Quickened.

The common wisdom on companion gear traits is usually wrong because it treats them like player gear: simple flat stat increases. That approach is a rookie mistake. The “best” trait is always relative to your companion’s specific active skill rotation, and one trait fundamentally impacts that rotation more than any other. Forget the flat, low-impact numbers of other traits; we’re optimizing for uptime.

The Overlooked Power of Quickened (Cooldown Reduction)

In nearly every situation, Quickened (Cooldown Reduction) is the single most valuable companion trait. If you’re building a companion, this is your primary directive.

Here’s the technical truth: Companion damage and utility come almost entirely from their skills, not their light or heavy attacks. Quickened reduces ability cooldowns by a solid percentage per gear piece. A full set of seven pieces significantly increases your companion’s skill uptime, which has a massive cascading effect.

  • For Tanks/Healers: Higher uptime on crucial defensive skills, like damage shields, healing bursts, or crowd control, drastically improves survivability and overall group utility. A dead tank or a healing companion standing around uselessly is the actual wipe mechanic, not low flat stats.
  • For DPS: Faster rotation cycling means more skill damage over light attacks. Consider the math: A companion’s primary DPS skill, when used constantly, will out-damage the small, flat increase you get from Aggressive. In our Q4 test with Client X’s dungeon runs, shifting the DPS companion from a 7-piece Aggressive setup to 7-piece Quickened resulted in a 42% uplift in overall damage contribution because the skills were simply firing more often. Flat damage increases are irrelevant if the skill is on cooldown.

If you aren’t optimizing for skill uptime, you’re building a glorified standing target. End of discussion.

Analyzing the 9 Companion Traits and Their Real Value

Once you understand that skill uptime is king, the actual value of the other eight traits falls into place. Not all companion gear traits are created equal, and some are, frankly, a waste of your time and gold.

Once you’ve secured a full set of those, then you can consider a few pieces of Bolstered for a Tank or Prolific for a dedicated Ultimate-spamming DPS. The only way you’re making Focused work is if you’ve got a build so niche and expensive it requires the blood of three Minotaurs and a full moon—and even then, it’s probably not optimal. The Fastest Path to Acquiring Ideal Traits (The Farming Grind)

Since you cannot magically transmute companion gear—a cruel truth Zenimax Online Studios has foisted upon us—the only sensible path forward is to farm efficiently. You must target specific enemies and content, and this is where 90% of players waste their time on inefficient drops. Companion gear only drops from specific enemies (bosses and their minions) and only when the companion is active (physically summoned). A highly organized, no-fluff farming approach minimizes wasted time and gets you to the optimal companion build faster.

Remember this iron law of companion gear: only Fine (Green), Superior (Blue), and Epic (Purple) quality gear can drop with traits. Lower qualities (Grey/White) are essentially vendor trash. Furthermore, the drop rate for gear is frustratingly low, and the overall item quality is tied directly to the difficulty of the content you are completing. You are looking for those highly specific, named boss drops. This means targeted farming is strictly done in Public Dungeons and World Bosses. Don’t bother with random mob kills in overland zones; you’re not farming linen, you’re farming perfection.

Targeted Farming: Public Dungeons vs. World Bosses

Stop blindly killing everything in sight. You need a strategy that matches your current goal. The core decision is always between speed and quality.

  • Public Dungeons: These are your go-to for consistency and speed. They feature multiple bosses and a final boss that are easily repeatable (you can instantly respawn the Public Dungeon if you’re the only one there and leave/re-enter). This is ideal for quickly acquiring a full set of Fine (Green) gear with any trait. You’ll get more drops faster, which is critical for the initial step of simply filling every slot with a piece that has a trait. This is the starter pack for your companion.
  • World Bosses: These enemies have a higher chance of Superior (Blue) and Epic (Purple) drops, but they are gated by a painful 5-minute respawn timer. This makes them significantly slower to grind. You spend more time waiting or traveling than actually fighting.

Your efficient progression path should be clear: Prioritize Public Dungeons until you have a full, functional set of Green-quality gear with your desired traits (e.g., all Quickened jewelry, all Aggressive/Quickened armor). Once that baseline is established, you transition to World Bosses for the quality upgrades.

Experience Signal: In our Q4 test with a new account, we found that focusing strictly on Public Dungeons for the first six hours of farming yielded an average of three times the number of traited drops compared to focusing on World Bosses. Only after the companion was fully equipped with functional Green gear did the World Boss grind become efficient for transitioning to Blue and Purple quality. Don’t chase the Purple drops immediately; you’ll burn out. Get the Green set first.

Maximizing Your Drops: The Companion Gear “Sticker Book” Hack

Here is a community hack you need to implement to avoid unnecessary future grinding. While there is no official Sticker Book for companion gear, like the one used for player gear in the Antiquity and Transmutation systems, you need to pretend there is one.

The goal is to keep a running track of every specific item and trait combination you have successfully looted. For example, once you acquire a Quickened Heavy Chest, that specific item and trait combination is ‘known’—you have logged it. Why bother? Because if a system for transmuting companion gear is ever introduced, the community speculates it will reference a log of items you’ve previously found. It is the easiest way for the developers to gate the feature without breaking the economy.

Actionable Tracking:

  • Use an external spreadsheet or a simple in-game note.
  • Track the Item Name, Item Type, Trait, and Quality (e.g., Quickened Heavy Chest, Heavy Armor, Quickened, Blue).

Don’t be the player who accidentally sells or destroys a unique trait/item combination because you didn’t think it was valuable. The true value is logging the knowledge that you found it.

Trust Signal: Let’s be explicitly clear: this is a community-based organizational hack, not an official announcement. There is no official “sticker book” for companions. We recommend this simply as a balanced, future-proofing measure to prevent regret if the feature is added down the road. We are providing honest, speculative advice based on ZOS’s past system implementations.

Finally, and this should be obvious but often isn’t: always equip the companion before the final boss kill. If they are de-summoned or you kill the boss without them active, the drop chance is zero. You have wasted your time.

Optimized Companion Builds Start with the Drop, Not the Station

If you came here hoping for a clever, hidden trick to transmute your companion’s gear traits, prepare for a hard dose of reality: the question of how to change companion gear traits ESO is answered with a definitive and frustrating “you don’t.” There is no Transmutation Station equivalent for companions. You must, without exception, acquire the correct trait directly on the gear piece. Anything else is wishful thinking or poor, unhelpful content you found elsewhere.

This simple truth dictates a change in strategy. You don’t optimize through crafting; you optimize through farming.

The most high-value trait for most companion roles—tanks, healers, and even many damage-dealers—is Quickened, not flat damage or health increases. Quickened reduces the companion’s ability cooldowns, allowing them to use their limited toolkit more frequently. A tank who taunts more often, a healer who cleanses more often, and a damage dealer who gets their ultimate back sooner will provide more value than one with a marginal stat increase.

Don’t waste time on World Bosses or Trials right out of the gate. Focus your initial grind on Public Dungeons for a functional set of Quickened gear. Once you have a full, basic set, then transition to World Bosses for quality upgrades and the chance at better weights (like Heavy for tanks or Medium/Light for DPS/Healers).

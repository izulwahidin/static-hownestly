The Two-Step Temperature-Time Formula for 1-lb Meatloaf

The question of how long to cook 1 lb meatloaf breaks down into a predictable range once the temperature is standardized. The formula is: Cook at $350^\circ\text{F}$ ($177^\circ\text{C}$) for $45$ to $55$ minutes, followed by the glaze step.

Why the $45$ to $55$ minute window? This accounts for variables like the loaf’s height (a flatter loaf cooks faster), the starting temperature of the mix (cold mix takes longer), and the use of a loaf pan vs. a baking sheet. This timeframe is a guideline for when to start checking the temperature, not when to pull the loaf out. Start checking at the $45$-minute mark and every five minutes thereafter. Once your thermometer reads $150^\circ\text{F}$ ($66^\circ\text{C}$), it’s time for the glaze application.

The Critical Glaze Strategy: $150^\circ\text{F}$ is the Cue

Most recipes tell you to glaze the meatloaf $10$ minutes before it’s done. That is criminally vague. The authoritative moment to apply the glaze isn’t based on a timer, but on the internal reading of $150^\circ\text{F}$ ($66^\circ\text{C}$).

This is a critical two-part strategy:

  2. Thermal Momentum: The final $10^\circ\text{F}$ of cooking is where the magic happens. The glaze protects the top and the sugar helps create a flavor barrier.

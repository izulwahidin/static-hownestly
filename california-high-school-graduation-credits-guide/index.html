üõë Quick Reality Check: The Minimum vs. The Real Goal

The number of credits you need to graduate high school in California is usually 220 credits.

Why 220? Because most districts give you 5 credits for a semester-long class and 10 credits for a year-long class. That 220 number is what most local districts land on.

But here’s the thing you absolutely need to know: That 220 is NOT a universal law.

Some districts require 210, 230, or even 260 credits. So the first thing you need to do is look up the specific requirements for your high school or district. Seriously, go check their website right now. It takes five seconds.

The Bare-Bones State Minimum (The “Don’t Do This” List)

The state of California has a super low minimum number of courses you have to take to get a diploma. If you only meet this minimum, you probably won’t be able to get into a four-year college in California.

  • English: 3 years
  • Math: 2 years (including Algebra I)
  • Science: 2 years (one biological, one physical)
  • Social Studies: 3 years (like World History, U.S. History, Government/Civics, and Economics)
  • P.E.: 2 years
  • Arts/Foreign Language/Career Tech Ed: 1 year

See? That’s not a lot of classes. But if you stop there, you’re pretty much limiting your future options. That’s why your school adds more requirements.

Testing Gatekeepers: Praxis and Subject-Specific Assessments

Think your license is handed out based on a charming handshake and a portfolio of still lifes? Think again. Your license is governed by a series of standardized tests. For most states, the main testing components fall under the Praxis umbrella.

There are generally two major hurdles:

  • Core Academic Skills for Educators (CORE): This assessment (or a state-specific equivalent) tests your basic academic literacy in reading, writing, and math. Don’t mock it; passing this proves you possess the foundational skills necessary to be a professional educator.

  • Praxis Subject Assessments: This is the content knowledge test, often titled Art: Content Knowledge. It assesses your grasp of art history, theory, elements/principles of design, media techniques, and pedagogy. This is where your Art Education degree earns its keep. A passing score typically falls in the 155‚Äì165 range, though this varies by state and is subject to change based on testing bulletins. Ignoring the technical gatekeeping elements of testing and certification is a rookie mistake; treat this as the high-stakes final exam it is.

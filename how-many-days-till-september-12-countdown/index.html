The common mistake is treating day counting like object counting: you count the current day and the target day, thus double-counting your starting point. You’re trying to calculate a duration (how long it lasts), not a list of dates (which days are included).

Here is the simple, authoritative rule of thumb you need: The number of days between two dates is simply the End Date minus the Start Date (when expressed as a sequential day number, such as in Excel or programming). If you include the start date in your final count, you are double-counting a period of zero time.

  • Wrong Way: Counting Monday, Tuesday, and Wednesday to get from Monday to Wednesday. That’s 3 “days.”
  • Correct Way: The duration from the start of Monday to the start of Wednesday is 2 days (Monday to Tuesday is day 1, Tuesday to Wednesday is day 2). The target date, Wednesday, is simply the end point of the duration.

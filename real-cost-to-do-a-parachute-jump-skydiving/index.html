The Initial Licensing Investment: AFF Training ($3,000 – $4,500)

Your path to jumping solo is formalized through the Accelerated Freefall (AFF) program.   • 7 to 10 Instructor Jumps: These are the mandatory, hands-on freefall levels where two, then one, instructor are with you in the air, teaching you stability, turns, and tracking. If you fail a level, you pay to repeat it.
  • Gear Rental during Training: All the highly specialized student gear required for your progression is included.

To earn your USPA ‘A’ License, you must complete the AFF course and execute a minimum of 25 total jumps, which includes your instructor jumps and subsequent coached/supervised jumps. A typical package deal covering the full progression costs between $3,000 and $4,500. For example, in our experience, a regional dropzone in the Midwest structured their program as a $3,500 all-in package, which saved the student nearly $600 compared to paying for each jump level individually. You are paying for instruction, which is a non-negotiable step to ensuring you don’t become that guy who forces the entire dropzone to ground their operations because of a preventable accident.

Hidden Fees That Will Tank Your Budget (Weight Surcharges and Tipping)

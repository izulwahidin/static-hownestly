Method 1: Google Maps (The “Freeze” Tactic)

Google Maps is tied heavily to the Internet connection. It does not notify the other person if your phone simply goes offline.

  1. Disable App Data Access: Go to Settings > Apps > Maps (or Google Maps).
  2. Restrict Background Data: Navigate to Mobile data & Wi-Fi (or similar) and turn off the toggles for both Background data and Unrestricted data usage.
  3. The Result: Your location, to the other person, will appear to freeze at the last known position. The time stamp for your location will simply stop updating. You haven’t “stopped sharing,” you’ve merely created a connectivity issue that looks like you went into a tunnel or lost service.

Method 2: Life360 (The “Driver” Kill Switch)

Life360 is notoriously aggressive, and its notifications are hard to bypass. Simply turning off Wi-Fi/data will often flag you as being “offline.” The best way to beat Life360 is to starve it of the data it needs to function in the background.

  1. Kill the App’s Permissions: Go to Settings > Apps > Life360.
  2. Disable Location Permission: Change the location permission from Allow all the time to Allow only while using the app‚Äîor, better, Ask every time. Life360 requires “all the time” access to function seamlessly.
  3. Force Stop: Now, hit the Force Stop button.

  • The Savvy Checker’s Diagnosis: A skilled observer knows that a phone that is genuinely dead or off will often show its last known location for a period, or, if enough time has passed, simply report “offline.” A phone that reports “Location Not Found” while the user is still active on social media or responding to texts is a manual flag. Why? Because it indicates the user is blocking the Find My app specifically, but the device is otherwise functional. The difference is subtle but lethal to your stealth.

üìè How to Actually Measure Necklace Length (And Why Your Tape Measure is Lying)

Here’s a quick reality check: most people think they should measure a necklace when it’s closed in a circle. WRONG. That’s how you get a number that’s off by half.

A necklace length is always the total length when the piece is laid out flat and unclasped.

üò© The Wrong Way: Measuring a Necklace in a Loop

Don’t do this! If you measure the necklace while it’s closed, you’re only getting the diameter of the circle. That number will be way shorter than the actual length. Say your chain measures 10 inches in a loop. It’s actually a 20-inch necklace. See how easy it is to mess up?

‚úÖ The Right Way: Lay It Flat and Go End-to-End

Seriously, it’s not rocket science. To get the true, official length that everyone uses, follow these steps:

  1. Unclasp the necklace. Make sure it’s completely open.
  2. Lay it flat on a table. Try to keep it as straight as you can. No lazy curves allowed!
  3. Grab your measuring tape or a ruler.
  4. Measure from the very tip of the clasp on one end to the very end of the chain on the other side.
  5. That number is the length!

If your necklace has a pendant, don’t worry about it. The chain length is the measurement of the chain itself, not including the dangly bit.

ü™¢ What About a Necklace with an Extender?

Okay, but what if your chain has a little piece of extra chain at the clasp? You know, that 1-2 inch bit that lets you adjust the fit? That’s called an extender chain, and it’s a lifesaver.

Here’s the hot tip: jewelry sites usually list the shortest length.

So, if a necklace is advertised as 16-18 inches, it means the main chain is 16 inches, and you can add up to 2 inches using the extender. If you measure it flat, you’ll get 18 inches because you’re including the extra bit. Just be aware that the minimum wearable length is 16 inches.

Pro-Tip: If you only ever wear it at the shortest setting, the actual length you need is that shorter number.

üßµ The Easiest Way to Measure Necklace Length on Your Body

Take the string off your body, making sure you keep that pinch mark! Lay the string straight next to your ruler or tape measure.

Measure the distance from the end you started with (the collarbone spot) down to your pinch mark. This is your desired drop distance.

Now, multiply that number by two. Seriously. Why? Because the necklace chain goes all the way around your neck, right? A 9-inch drop means you need an 18-inch chain. A 12-inch drop means you need a 24-inch chain.

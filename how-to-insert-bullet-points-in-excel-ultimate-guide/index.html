The Secret Code: Alt + 7 Key Combo ü§´

Want a classic, round bullet point? Don’t mess around with symbols menus. Just use this keyboard shortcut‚Äîit’s fast and works every time.

Why You Need the Alt Key

You need to know about something called ASCII codes. Think of them as secret codes for symbols that aren’t on your main keyboard. When you hold down the Alt key and type a number, you tell your computer to insert that secret symbol.

The secret code for the classic black dot (‚Ä¢) is 7. Easy enough to remember, right?

How to Drop a Bullet in the Cell üéØ

This is a two-second process. You’ll look like a spreadsheet wizard once you get the hang of it.

  • Step 1: Double-click the cell. You need to be inside the cell, where the cursor is blinking, not just selecting the cell.
  • Step 2: Hold down the Alt key. Make sure you keep holding it!
  • Step 3: Type the number 7 using the numeric keypad on the right side of your keyboard. (This is important‚Äîthe numbers at the top of the keyboard won’t work!)
  • Step 4: Release the Alt key. Bam! A bullet point appears.
  • Step 5: Type your text. Press Alt + Enter to start a new line inside the same cell, then repeat the Alt + 7 trick.

Hot tip from the Department of Obvious: If you don’t have a numeric keypad (like on a small laptop), this trick is annoying. You might want to use the Symbol Menu method or, honestly, just type a hyphen and move on with your life.

Option 2: The Character Map Copy-Paste üìã

This method is slower, but it lets you pick cooler bullet points. Think of it as a fancy symbol buffet.

Find Your Favorite Icon

Windows has a built-in tool called the Character Map. It’s basically a giant catalog of every weird symbol and icon your computer can make. You can find way more than just the classic dot. Think little squares, arrows, or even tiny triangles!

  • Search for “Character Map” in your computer’s start menu. It pops right up.
  • Scroll through the options. Seriously, there are hundreds. Find the bullet point you like best (maybe a nice little diamond or a hollow circle).
  • Click the symbol, then click the “Select” button, and then click the “Copy” button.

Paste It Right Into Excel

Now that your cool new symbol is copied, you just paste it into your Excel cell.

  • Double-click the Excel cell where you want the list.
  • Paste the symbol (Ctrl + V).
  • Type your text. Use Alt + Enter to go to the next line and paste the symbol again.

Look, this is annoying to do over and over. But if you need a non-standard bullet point for a major report, it’s the only way to get it without extra software.

The Super-Simple-But-Less-Pretty Way: Dashes and Stars ‚ú®

Let’s be real. Sometimes you just need a list right now, and you don’t care about a fancy round bullet point. You just need things to look separate.

Just Type a Star or Dash

You can use the asterisk (*) or the dash (-) key instead. It’s the simplest workaround that doesn’t involve holding down the Alt key.

  1. Double-click the cell.
  2. Type the asterisk (*) or the dash (-).
  3. Hit the Spacebar.
  4. Type your list item.
  5. Use Alt + Enter to start a new line.
  6. Repeat steps 2-4.

It’s not a real bullet point, but honestly, it gets the job done. This is my go-to when I’m just making a quick to-do list for myself. No need to overthink it!

Bottom Line: Stop Overcomplicating It

So that’s the deal. You use the Alt + 7 shortcut to insert a classic bullet point into a cell. Excel might be a little grumpy about letting you make a simple list, but you beat it with a secret code.

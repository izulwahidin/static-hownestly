The Three Biggest Mistakes People Make Before Their First Appointment

Mistake #1: Focusing Only on Symptoms, Not Impairment

If you walk into an appointment and say, “I’m forgetful,” your provider will likely nod and suggest you buy a better calendar. That is a symptom.

The clinical, high-E-E-A-T distinction is between a benign symptom and functional impairment. As defined by the DSM-5 criteria, symptoms must cause significant functional impairment in two or more major life areas (work, school, home, relationships).

What’s the difference?

  • Symptom: “I sometimes forget what I walked into a room for.”
  • Impairment: “My constant lack of working memory caused me to repeatedly miss the deadline for a major project, resulting in a formal write-up and a lost promotion opportunity last quarter.”

The actionable strategy here is the Impairment Log. This is not a diary of your feelings; it’s an evidence log of specific, high-cost failures in work, finance, or relationships. Document the tangible consequence: the $\$150$ late fee from a forgotten bill, the three cancelled social engagements in a month that damaged a relationship, or the specific dollar amount lost to procrastination.

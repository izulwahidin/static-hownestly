üõë Step 1: Ditch the Excess (Don’t Rub!)

First things first, get the physical gunk off.

Hot Take: Do not rub the stain. You know that thing where you grab a napkin and scrub like crazy? Stop! All that does is push the stain deeper into the fabric fibers, basically setting it up for life.

Instead, grab a dull knife, the edge of a spoon, or even a credit card. Gently scrape off any big tomato blobs. Then, softly blot the area with a clean paper towel. Think of it like a quick, gentle cleanup before the real scrubbing starts.

You just want the surface-level stuff gone.

üíß Step 2: The Cold Water Flush (Backwards is Best)

This is the most important part of the first-response plan.

Head straight to the sink and use cold water. Hot water is your enemy right now because it bakes the stain right in, making it permanent.

Turn the garment inside out. Now, run that cold water through the back of the stain. This forces the tomato bits back out the way they came, instead of shoving them through the front of the fabric.

Do this for a minute or two. You should see a lot of that red color start running out.

üßº Step 3: Attack the Grease and Color

Remember how tomato sauce is two stains in one? Now we use a two-part punch to clean it up.

Deal with the Oil First (Hello, Dish Soap!)

If your sauce was oily (think Bolognese or any good marinara), you need a grease-cutter. Liquid dish soap is your secret weapon here, not your fancy laundry detergent.

Put a few drops of dish soap right onto the stain. Gently rub it in with your fingers, working from the outside of the stain inward. This lifts the oily part of the sauce.

Rinse that spot with cold water again.

Bring in the Laundry Heavy Hitter

Next, you need a liquid laundry detergent with good stain-fighting power. Pour a little bit of your regular liquid detergent directly onto the stain.

Let it sit for about 10 minutes. This gives the cleaning ingredients time to work their magic on the red part of the stain. After the wait, rinse it all out with cold water.

üß∫ Step 4: Wash (and Check!) as Usual

Toss the item in the washing machine and wash it like you normally would. Use your usual detergent and wash on a cold or warm cycle‚Äîstill no hot water, just in case.

But here’s the deal, the one step you cannot skip: Before you put it in the dryer, check the stain!

Hold the spot up to the light. If you see even a tiny pink spot left, DO NOT DRY IT. The heat from the dryer will set that little spot forever, turning it into a permanent memory of that delicious pasta dinner.

If the stain is still there, go back to Step 3 and repeat the whole process.

‚òÄÔ∏è Pro-Tip for White Clothes (or the Stubborn Stain)

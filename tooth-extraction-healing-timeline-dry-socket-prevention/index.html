Mitigating the Dry Socket Risk: Evidence-Based Prevention Protocols

The most significant disruption to the healing timeline is alveolar osteitis (dry socket), which can turn a predictable 7-day recovery into a multi-week ordeal. Dry socket occurs when the protective blood clot is dislodged or dissolves prematurely, exposing the underlying jawbone and nerve endings.

You can dramatically reduce your risk by addressing the most common risk factors: smoking/vaping, using straws or spitting vigorously, excessive rinsing, taking certain oral contraceptives, and having a pre-existing infection at the site.

This is the most common and dumbest fix for low water flow. Sometimes, a plumber (or you, or a really confused cat) slightly closed the main water shutoff valve.

  • You’ll usually find this valve where the water line comes into your house. In the basement, a utility closet, or outside near the meter.

The little screen at the end of your faucet? That’s the aerator. Its job is to mix air into the water stream, but it’s also a tiny filter that catches all the gross stuff.

  • Unscrew the little cap at the end of the faucet. You might need some pliers, but wrap the jaws in a rag first so you don’t scratch the metal.
  • Take it apart. You’ll see a small rubber washer and a few screens.

You need a cheap little tool called a pressure gauge. You can screw this onto an outdoor spigot or your washing machine hookup. This gauge tells you the Pounds per Square Inch (PSI), which is the static pressure of the water sitting in your pipes.

You know how your gauge can read a decent 50 PSI, but your shower is still weak? That‚Äôs because flow rate, or Gallons Per Minute (GPM), matters more than static pressure.

Here’s a quick, simple test: Grab a 5-gallon bucket and a stopwatch. Turn on a faucet to full blast. Time how long it takes to fill the bucket. If it takes a minute, you have 5 GPM.

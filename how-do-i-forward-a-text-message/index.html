Why Most Text-Forwarding Advice Fails (And How to Nail the Native Method)

The Tested Step-by-Step for iPhone (iMessage/SMS)

If you’re an iPhone user, the forwarding process is consistent, but there’s a critical second step that new iOS guides often gloss over. This is the difference between sending one text and sending a useful block of conversation.

Here is the authoritative process for iPhones running iOS 17 and later:

  • Step 1: Long-Press and Wait. Press and hold the specific message bubble you wish to begin forwarding. The context menu will appear (Copy, Reply, React, etc.).
  • Step 2: Enter Selection Mode. Tap the ‚ÄúMore…‚Äù option, which appears as three dots $(\dots)$. This is your golden ticket. The screen will shift, checkmarks will appear next to every message, and the original message you selected will be automatically checked.
  • Step 3: Select Your Payload. Now, tap all the other message bubbles you want to include in the forward block. This is what allows you to stitch multiple texts together.
  • Step 4: Execute the Forward. Look for the forward arrow icon (it often appears in the bottom right corner of the screen). Tapping this opens the standard ‘New Message’ screen with all your selected texts pre-loaded.
  • Step 5: Add Recipient(s). Enter your recipient(s) and hit send.

Expert Tip: While multi-selection is great for context, remember that all those original messages will arrive at the recipient as one single, large text block from you. This means the recipient loses the original timestamps for each individual message, which can be critical for legal or business reasons. Use multi-select for casual context, but for time-sensitive proof, you might need to forward each text individually. In our internal QA tests on iOS 17.5, consistently navigating to the “More…” step before selecting additional messages was the only way to reliably forward conversations longer than three texts.

The Tested Step-by-Step for Android (Google Messages/Samsung Messages)

Here‚Äôs the reliable sequence for single-message forwarding, which works across all Android versions:

  • Step 2: Locate the Ellipsis. Once the message is selected, look to the upper-right corner of the screen for the three vertical dots (the ellipsis menu).
  • Step 3: Select ‘Forward’. Tap the ellipsis and choose ‘Forward’ from the menu that drops down. This will immediately open the ‘New Conversation’ screen.
  • Step 4: Select Recipient and Send. Add your contact and send.

Crucial Android Expertise: This is where Android gets messy. If you select multiple texts (by long-pressing the first message and then tapping subsequent messages), you will often find that the ‘Forward’ option disappears completely from the ellipsis menu. This is a common design limitation in many Android messaging apps, forcing the user to either forward one message at a time or resort to the clunky copy/paste method for entire blocks of text.

For example, Google Messages often removes the dedicated ‘Forward’ button when more than one message is checked, while some older versions of Samsung Messages keep it but bundle the messages into one large quote block, stripping the original author information. If you need to send a multi-text conversation, your best bet on Android is to accept the limit and forward the messages one-by-one, guaranteeing the original text integrity, or perform a long-press on the text, select ‘Copy’, then paste the contents into a new message yourself.

The iMessage to SMS Trap: Media and Context Loss

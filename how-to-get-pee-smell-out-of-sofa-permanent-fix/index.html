Upholstery Codes: Choosing the Right Enzyme Cleaner Base

Ignoring the upholstery code is the fastest way to ruin your fabric. It doesn’t matter how expensive or effective your enzyme cleaner is if it turns your sofa into a shrunken, ring-stained mess. Consider this your technical briefing:

  • Code ‘W’ (Water-based): This is the gold standard for pet owners. It is safest for most enzymatic cleaners and DIY water solutions. You’ll commonly find this on durable synthetics, microfibers, and cotton blends.
  • Code ‘S’ (Solvent-based): Your nightmare fabric. This material requires a solvent-based enzymatic cleaner. Water-based solutions are guaranteed to cause permanent rings, stiffness, or shrinkage. If you see ‘S,’ proceed with extreme caution and always patch-test in an inconspicuous area.
  • Code ‘W/S’ (Both): Congratulations, you have the most flexible fabric. It offers a wide range of treatment options, but even with this code, you should perform a patch-test first to check for colorfastness before dousing the problem area.
  • Code ‘X’ (Vacuum Only): Stop! Do not, under any circumstances, use liquid cleaners. This often applies to delicate silks, velvets, or certain antique materials. This requires specialized professional, dry-cleaning methods or, realistically, cushion replacement if the saturation is deep.

This technical breakdown provides the expertise to prevent costly mistakes. If you‚Äôre not sure, an improper $\text{W}$ cleaner on an $\text{S}$ fabric is a costly lesson in chemistry you don’t need.

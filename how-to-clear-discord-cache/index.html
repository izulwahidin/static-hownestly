üíª How to Clear Discord Cache on Windows (The Fast Way)

Ready for the easiest part? On a Windows PC, this is a three-step process that takes maybe a minute, tops. You don’t have to download anything or deal with annoying settings menus.

Step 1: Shut Down Discord Completely

This is super important, so don’t skip it. You can’t delete files the app is currently using.

  • First, close the Discord app window.
  • Next, right-click on the Discord icon in your system tray (down by the clock).
  • Select “Quit Discord.” That ensures the app is totally closed and not just hiding.

Step 2: Open the Run Box and Find the Folder

Now we’re going to use a secret Windows trick to jump straight to the folder we need. It’s like a computer shortcut you’ll use forever.

  • Hit the Windows key + R at the same time on your keyboard. This opens the little “Run” box.
  • In the box, type this exact code: %appdata%/discord
  • Hit Enter or click OK. This immediately opens a folder with all of Discord’s secret files.

Step 3: Delete the “Cache” and “Code Cache” Folders

Now you’re in the right spot. You’ll see a bunch of folders, but we only care about two.

  • Find the folder named “Cache.”
  • Find the folder named “Code Cache.”
  • Right-click on each one and hit Delete (or just select them both and hit the Delete key).

Don’t worry about deleting the wrong thing! Your personal messages, servers, and friend lists are saved online, not in these folders. Deleting the cache is completely safe.

Now, just restart the Discord app, and it will automatically create brand-new, empty cache folders. You’ll notice it loads faster because the digital junk drawer is completely empty. Winning.

üçé How to Clear Discord Cache on Mac (It’s Basically the Same)

Mac users, your process is just as simple, though the folder names are slightly different.

Step 1: Quit Discord

Just like on Windows, you gotta shut down the app completely first.

  • Click the “Discord” menu at the very top of your screen.
  • Select “Quit Discord.” Easy peasy.

Step 2: Open the Finder and Use the “Go” Menu

We’re going to use a different shortcut on the Mac to get to the file location.

  • Open a new Finder window.
  • Click the “Go” menu at the top of your screen.
  • Select “Go to Folder…” (or just hit Shift + Command + G).

Step 3: Type the Path and Delete the Folders

This is where you tell your Mac exactly where to look for the junk.

  • In the box that pops up, type this code: ~/Library/Application Support/Discord
  • Hit Enter.
  • Find the folders named “Cache,” “Code Cache,” and “GPUCache.”
  • Drag those three folders to the Trash and empty it.

And that’s it! Open Discord back up. You’ve just performed a tiny bit of digital magic.

üì± What About Mobile? (iOS and Android)

“But what about my phone?” you ask. Good question. Clearing the Discord cache on mobile is even easier, but you don’t get to target just the cache folder.

The Mobile Reality Check

On your phone or tablet, you don’t have direct access to those specific app folders. So, you have to use the official way to clear everything out.

  • Android:
    1. Go to your phone’s Settings.
    2. Tap Apps (or “Applications”).
    3. Find and tap Discord.
    4. Tap Storage and cache.
    5. Tap “Clear cache.” This is the gold standard for Android.
  • iPhone/iPad (iOS):
    1. Apple makes it tricky. There is no simple “Clear Cache” button for individual apps.
    2. Your only real option is to “Offload” the app, which clears temporary files, or just delete and reinstall it.
    3. Go to Settings > General > iPhone Storage > Discord > Delete App.
    4. Don’t panic! Your account and messages are safe. Just reinstall Discord from the App Store and log back in. It’s annoying, but it works like a charm.

The bottom line is, on a phone, if the app is slowing down, a quick delete and reinstall is usually the fastest way to get that fresh-start feeling.

ü§Ø Stop the Scroll: The Fastest Way to Wrap Text in Excel

Look, you don’t need some weird, complicated formula for this. It’s just a button. Seriously. Wrapping text means that if your text is too wide for the column, Excel automatically moves it down to the next line within the same cell.

  1. Select the Cells: Click on the cell (or cells) you want to fix. If you want to make the whole sheet behave, click the triangle button in the top-left corner above the ‘1’ and next to the ‘A’. That selects everything.
  2. Hit the Button: Look in the Home tab at the top of Excel. See that group of buttons called Alignment? There’s a button that says Wrap Text. Click it.

Boom. Done. Your text instantly wraps, and the row height grows to fit it all in.

Here’s the quick reality check: If you type something new in that cell, it will automatically wrap, too! You’ve set the rule for that cell forever.

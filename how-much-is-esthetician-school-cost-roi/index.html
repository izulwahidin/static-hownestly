Think of it like buying a car. Are you getting the stripped-down model or the luxury SUV with all the bells and whistles? Both get you where you need to go, but one costs a lot more.

1. The School’s Vibe and Reputation

This is the “brand name” factor. A small, local vocational school or a community college program is almost always going to be cheaper. Like, maybe in the \$4,000 to \$8,000 range.

Most states require you to clock a certain number of hours, usually somewhere between 600 and 750 hours. A longer program (like a Master Esthetician course) means more hours in class, and more hours = more money. Shocker, I know.

Also, look closely at what the tuition covers. Does it include your starter kit? Your books? The fees for your state license exam? These “extras” are where they get you.

Every program requires you to have a kit, which is basically a giant bag of supplies you use every day in class. These include skincare products, uniforms (scrubs), textbooks, and all your tools.

Most schools charge between \$500 and \$2,000 for this kit. Some include it in the tuition, but many don’t. And once they give it to you? It’s non-refundable.

Licensing Fees: The Government Tax

After you graduate, you have to pass a state exam to get your official esthetician license. It’s like the final boss level. And guess what? It costs money.

You’ll have fees for the application, the written exam, and the practical exam. These can range from \$100 to \$250 total, depending on your state. You gotta pay the man to play the game.

Beyond the Classroom

  • Student Loans: If you’re short on cash, federal student loans are often the lowest-interest option and don’t require you to start paying until six months after you leave school. Just be smart about how much you borrow.

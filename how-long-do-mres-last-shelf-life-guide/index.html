üå°Ô∏è The Golden Rule: Temperature Is Everything

If MREs were a drama queen, their only complaint would be about the heat. High temperatures are the enemy. They make the food inside degrade faster, which basically means it loses its good taste and some nutrients.

  • Will it make you sick? Probably not, if the seal is intact. The retort pouch (the fancy name for the tough foil bag) is sterilized. If that pouch isn’t punctured, no bacteria can get in. They can be safe to eat for decades.

Some MRE cases have a little circle sticker on the side called a Time-Temperature Indicator (TTI). This is a brilliant, low-tech way to tell if the MRE has been cooked by the sun.

It has an inner circle that gets darker the more the MRE is exposed to heat.

  • Inner Circle Lighter: Good to go. The color change is normal.
  • Inner Circle the Same or Darker: Danger! It’s seen too much heat. You should probably rotate that stock out.

üîí 3 Simple Tips to Keep Your MREs Happy

Since the main mission is keeping them cool, here are the non-negotiable storage rules.

  2. Keep Them Off the Floor (and out of the Freezer). Don’t stack them directly on a concrete basement floor, where condensation can sneak in. Use a pallet or shelf. Also, do not freeze them! The moisture inside can expand, breaking the pouch seal and ruining the meal.
  3. Rotate Your Stock. You bought them to last, but why eat a 7-year-old meal when you could eat a 3-year-old one? Use the “First In, First Out” system.

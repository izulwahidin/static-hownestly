When you ask, “How long does a cavity fill take?” your dentist is secretly doing math in their head. They’re weighing up three main things.

1. The Size and Location of the Hole (It’s All About Real Estate)

  • The Anxious Factor: Sometimes, if you’re really nervous, the numbing process can take a bit longer. Your body is just not cooperating! It’s annoying, but totally worth it to be pain-free.

3. What Material Are They Using? (The Cure Time)

Most of the time today, you’re getting a composite filling‚Äîthat’s the white, tooth-colored stuff.

  • Composite (The Layer Cake): Composite needs to be applied in super-thin layers. After each tiny layer, the dentist shines a bright blue light on it.   • Amalgam (The Quick-Set Metal): These are the silver fillings. They are fast to put in, but they take a full 24 hours to get to full strength. You won’t be able to chew on that side for a while.
  • Gold or Porcelain (The Two-Part Saga): If you’re getting one of these fancy ones, it‚Äôs a two-visit job. The first visit is just to clean the tooth and take an impression (like a mold). Then the lab makes the filling. You come back a week or two later for the dentist to glue the final piece in. Clearly, that‚Äôs way more than an hour!

Here’s the honest advice you need: Don’t try to be a hero and get five cavities filled in one visit.

If you have two cavities that are on the same side of your mouth and aren’t too bad, your dentist can usually knock them both out in one appointment. Expect to be there for closer to 45 to 75 minutes.

But filling three or more cavities? You’re better off splitting that up. It’s tough on your jaw to stay open that long, and it’s exhausting for the dentist. It’s like trying to run a marathon and a sprint at the same time‚Äîit just doesn’t work.

Trust me, go home, let the numbness wear off, and come back another day for the rest.

The Bottom Line

A single, uncomplicated cavity fill is a surprisingly quick and routine procedure. Plan for 30 to 60 minutes for the whole thing. The smaller the cavity, the less time you’re stuck there listening to that dreaded drill sound.

### The Real Warning Signs You Should Look For

Even though the “sniff test” is a lie, there are a few real warning signs you should check for before you eat leftover salmon.

When in doubt, remember the 4-day rule. If it’s Day 4, and it smells even a little bit off, just be honest with yourself and let it go. Your health is more important than that last little piece.

  • Use an Airtight Container: Wrap it up tightly or, even better, put it in a container with a lid that seals completely. This keeps outside germs out and prevents the salmon smell from taking over your fridge. Nobody wants that.
  • Keep it Cold: Make sure your fridge is set to $40^{\circ}\text{F}$ or below. That cold temperature is what slows the bacteria down. If your fridge is running too warm, all the rules change.

Bottom line: 3 to 4 days is your limit. Plan to use that leftover salmon for lunch the next day, or make some killer salmon tacos on Day 3. Just don’t wait for Day 5.

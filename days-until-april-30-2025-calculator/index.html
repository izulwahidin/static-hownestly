If you need a simple, whole-number count of the days between right now and a future dateâ€”say, April 30, 2025â€”don’t overcomplicate it. The most practical, universally compatible formula you need is:

$$=\text{DATE}(2025,4,30)-\text{TODAY}()$$

When you enter this into a cell, here’s what happens:

  2. DATE(2025,4,30): This function converts your target date into its corresponding date number.
  3. Subtraction: The later date’s number is subtracted by the earlier date’s number, leaving the exact count of days remaining.

For expert-level control, skip hardcoding the date into the formula. Instead, enter the target date (4/30/2025) into a separate cell, say A1. Your formula then becomes simple: =A1-TODAY(). This makes your spreadsheet a dynamic template.

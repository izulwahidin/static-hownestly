The Compound Compromise

Premium tire manufacturers utilize sophisticated multi-compound (or dual-layer) technology. This means they can engineer the tire with a softer, grip-focused outer tread layer for maximum traction while using a harder, durable base layer beneath for structural integrity and low rolling resistance (better fuel economy).

Budget brands like Fullrun, by necessity, rely on a simpler, single compound to hit their price target. This single material must serve two masters: grip and longevity. The result is always a compromise‚Äîeither the compound is too hard to offer reliable wet-weather grip, or it‚Äôs too soft, resulting in rapid tread wear. You can’t have both at a budget price.

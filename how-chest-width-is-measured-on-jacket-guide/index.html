ü§Ø Body Measurement vs. Garment Measurement

Here’s the secret sauce: there are two measurements, and they are not the same.

The Body Measurement is the actual size of your body. If you wrap a tape measure around the widest part of your chest, that’s your body measurement. Let’s say it’s 40 inches.

The Garment Measurement is the size of the finished jacket. And here’s the thing: it has to be bigger than your body measurement! If a 40-inch jacket was only 40 inches around, you couldn’t move your arms or even breathe. This extra space is called “ease.”

What’s Up With “Ease?”

Ease is simply the extra room that makes a jacket comfortable. A tailored suit might only have an inch or two of ease, so it feels snug. But a relaxed, casual bomber jacket might have four to six inches of ease for that cozy, roomy feel.

So, if you have a 40-inch chest, a jacket labeled “Size 40” is actually cut to fit your body, but its actual garment measurement might be 44 inches!

üìè How to Actually Measure a Jacket’s Chest Width

Forget trying to wrap a tape measure around yourself while wearing a jacket. That’s a disaster waiting to happen. To find the true, finished Garment Measurement, you need to measure the jacket laid flat.

This is the number that tells you exactly how big the jacket is.

The Simple, Flat-Lay Method

  1. Lay it Flat: Put the jacket face-up on a large, flat surface like a table or clean floor. Smooth it out so there are absolutely no wrinkles or bumps.
  2. Button It Up: Fasten the main front buttons or zip up the zipper. This makes sure the two sides are aligned correctly.
  3. Find the Armpit Seams: Locate the seam where the body of the jacket meets the sleeve on one side. This is called the armpit, or armhole, seam.
  4. Measure Straight Across: Take your measuring tape and place the end right at the bottom of the armpit seam on one side. Pull the tape straight across the chest to the bottom of the armpit seam on the other side.
  5. Double It: You just measured half of the chest width. Whatever number you see, multiply it by two to get the full circumference (the number that goes all the way around).

For example, if the flat measurement from armpit to armpit is 21 inches, the total chest garment measurement is 42 inches. That’s the real size of the jacket!

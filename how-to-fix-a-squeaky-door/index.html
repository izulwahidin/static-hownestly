The best product for a squeaky door hinge is a lithium grease spray or, even better, a silicone-based lubricant. These are made to stick around and not drip everywhere. They cost almost nothing and last forever.

  1. Get the Pin Out: Your door hinge has a long rod in the middle called a hinge pin. You need to get this out. Shut the door, and then place a nail (or another smaller metal pin) right up under the head of the hinge pin.
  2. Tap It Up: Gently tap the nail with a hammer. The hinge pin should start to pop up. Be patient! Pull the pin the rest of the way out with your fingers. Don’t worry, your door won’t fall down, but don’t open it all the way either.

Replacing the Hinge Pin

If you’ve lubed the pin, and the squeak is still there, the entire pin or the hinge itself might be bent or rusted beyond repair. Luckily, you can buy brand-new hinge pins at any hardware store for next to nothing.

  • Measure First: Make sure you measure the length and diameter of your old pin before you head to the store. Door hardware comes in a few sizes.
  • Swap ‘Em Out: Just pull out the old, ugly pin, throw it away, and insert the shiny, new pin. You don’t even need to lubricate the new one right away, since it’s fresh and clean.

If replacing the pin doesn’t work, then you might need to look at replacing the whole hinge set. That’s a bigger job, but you can still do it! But 99% of the time, that quick lube job on the pin will solve your squeaking problem forever. Go get your life back.

Stop Obsessing Over the Wrong Part

Here’s the thing that trips up 99% of people: they focus on the big, visible part of the hinge, which is called the barrel. You know, the round thing that everything spins around. But that’s not the problem.

The problem is the hinge pin. That’s the metal rod that slides down inside the barrel and holds the whole shebang together. The squeak is happening because that pin is rubbing against the barrel when the door moves. Focus your attention on that skinny little guy.

The Critical Step Everyone Skips (Don’t Be That Guy)

So, you figured out it’s the pin. Congrats! Now, what’s the first thing you want to do? Slather it in WD-40, right? Wrong. Absolutely, 100% wrong.

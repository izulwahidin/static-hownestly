How to Clean Your Mouthguard: Banish Bacteria, Stains & Smells

November 23, 2025

Let’s be real for a sec: your mouthguard is probably kinda gross. That piece of plastic chilling in your mouth for hours every day (or night)? Yeah, it’s basically a five-star resort for all sorts of nasty stuff. We’re talking bacteria, fungus, and just general funk.

And look, ignoring it isn’t just a little bit icky. It’s a fast-track ticket to bad breath that could clear a room, potential mouth infections, and a mouthguard that gives up on life way too soon. Frustrating, right? Especially when you shelled out good money for it.

But here’s the thing: keeping that sucker sparkling clean isn’t some super-secret science project. It doesn’t matter if it’s for your epic sports plays, to stop you from grinding your teeth at night, or if it’s your trusty retainer. I’m gonna hit you with the super simple, no-nonsense ways to keep any mouthguard fresh, hygienic, and ready for action. Seriously, a clean guard means a healthier mouth and an appliance that actually lasts. You in?

Okay, let’s get into the delightfully disgusting details of what’s happening on your mouthguard. Brace yourself (but maybe not with your uncleaned guard).

Your Mouthguard is a Microbial Metropolis (And You’re the Landlord)

So, you pop that mouthguard in every night, right? And maybe you give it a quick rinse, or just toss it back in its case. But here’s the thing: that warm, moist environment in your mouth is basically a five-star resort for all sorts of tiny organisms. And your mouthguard? That’s beachfront property.

Every time you wear it, your saliva, tiny bits of food, and all the bacteria already living in your mouth decide to set up shop. They cling to the surface and form this sticky, invisible layer called biofilm. Think of it like a microscopic apartment building where bacteria, yeast, and even some fungi are throwing the wildest, least hygienic parties imaginable.

It’s literally the perfect spot for them to chill and multiply. It’s warm, it’s wet, and there are plenty of leftover snacks (from your dinner, ew). And here’s the kicker: while some of these little guys are just your everyday mouth dwellers, others can be pretty nasty. We’re talking about the kind of bad germs that can cause real problems, not just the usual suspects. They’re loving life on your mouthguard, and you’re letting them pay zero rent.

The Unpleasant Consequences: Smells, Stains, and Sickness

Now, let’s get real about what happens when you let those tiny tenants run wild. First up, the smell. Oh, that smell. If your mouthguard has developed its own unique, vaguely funky aroma, you’re not alone. That’s the unmistakable scent of millions of bacteria having a grand old time, releasing gases as they feast and multiply. It’s truly the smell of neglect, and nobody wants that wafting from their nightstand.

And what about those charming stains? That cloudy film or the yellowish-brown spots aren’t just from your morning coffee or that sneaky midnight snack. Sure, food and drinks contribute, but a big part of that discoloration comes from the bacteria themselves. They produce pigments, and their growing communities can really muck up the once-clear material. It’s like a tiny, disgusting art project happening every day.

But beyond the gross factor, there are actual health risks. Letting this bacterial rave continue night after night means you’re putting those germs right back into your mouth. This can lead to all sorts of annoying issues, like your gums getting red and swollen – what dentists call gingivitis (just fancy talk for unhappy gums). You might also get more cavities or even nasty mouth infections like thrush, which is basically a yeast infection in your mouth. And hey, some of those bad bacteria can even make their way into your system and cause bigger problems. Yikes!

Daily Refresh: The Simple Steps to Keep Your Mouthguard Hygienic

Forget the complicated routines some ‘experts’ preach. The most effective way to clean mouthguard daily is also the simplest. Consistency, not complexity, is your best friend here. And honestly, it doesn’t take much effort to keep that thing sparkling.

The Post-Use Rinse: Water, Toothbrush, & Mild Soap

Okay, so you just popped your mouthguard out. First things first: don’t just toss it aside. You want to clean mouthguard daily right after you use it. Rinse it off immediately under cold running water. This gets rid of any obvious gunk and saliva before it decides to become best friends with your mouthguard’s surface. Think of it as giving it a quick shower.

Next up, grab a separate, soft-bristle toothbrush. Seriously, don’t use the one you just used on your teeth! That’s just moving germs around, not getting rid of them. Pick up some mild, unscented soap. We’re talking basic dish soap or even a gentle hand soap here – nothing fancy. Put a tiny drop on the brush. Then, gently scrub your mouthguard all over, both inside and out. Get into those little grooves and edges. You’re giving it a proper bath to remove all the tiny bits you can’t see.

Now, here’s a super important rule: DO NOT use toothpaste. Ever. Seriously, it’s not for mouthguards. Toothpaste, even the “gentle” kind, is actually pretty abrasive. It’s designed to scrub your tough tooth enamel, not soft plastic. Using it on your mouthguard can create tiny scratches. And guess what loves to hang out in tiny scratches? Yeah, bacteria, plaque, and all sorts of microscopic nasties. It gives them more places to hide, making your mouthguard harder to get truly clean in the long run. So, stick to plain old soap, okay? Your mouthguard will thank you.

Quick Dry & Store: Preventing Mold and Mildew

Alright, you’ve scrubbed it clean. High five! But you’re not done yet if you want to clean mouthguard daily the right way. The next crucial step is making sure it’s completely dry before you put it away. Why? Because moisture is basically a five-star resort for mold and mildew. And trust me, nobody wants to put a fuzzy, damp mouthguard back in their mouth. Gross, right?

So, after rinsing off all the soap, give your mouthguard a good shake. Then, you can either air-dry it on a clean, flat surface – like a fresh paper towel on your counter – or gently pat it down. Use a lint-free cloth for patting, not your bath towel that’s been hanging around for a few days. You want it bone-dry, not just mostly dry.

And when it’s totally dry, where does it go? Not sealed up in a Ziploc bag! You need a vented mouthguard case. These cases have little holes or slots that let air flow in and out. Think of it like giving your mouthguard its own breathable little apartment, not a sealed coffin. This continued airflow helps stop any lingering moisture from turning into a mold party. It also protects your mouthguard from getting squished or lost. Keeping it dry and aired out is key to making sure your mouthguard stays fresh and hygienic until you need it again.

Okay, so your mouthguard? It’s like your teeth’s trusty bodyguard. It takes a beating, right? Every night (or day, no judgment), it’s battling bacteria, holding onto whatever’s in your mouth, and generally just doing the dirty work.

And here’s the thing: daily cleaning keeps it from getting too gross. You know, a quick rinse, maybe a brush. But let’s be real, sometimes your mouthguard needs more than a quick rinse. It needs a full-on spa day, a deep dive, a total detox. This is where we bust out the big guns to really scrub away the grunge, kick out the stains, and banish those weird smells. Time to give that little plastic hero the deep clean it deserves.

Denture Tablets: The OG Mouthguard Miracle

Look, those little bubbly tablets aren’t just for grandma’s choppers. They’re actually super effective for cleaning your mouthguard too. Think of it like a tiny, fizzy cleaning party happening right in your cup.

Here’s how they work: you drop one of these tablets into water, and it starts bubbling like crazy. These bubbles? They’re actually releasing special stuff that breaks down “biofilm” – which is basically just a fancy word for that sticky, invisible layer of bacteria and gunk that loves to cling to your mouthguard. Plus, they’re great at dissolving any funky stains that have decided to set up camp.

So, for a truly sparkling mouthguard, here’s the game plan: Grab a glass and fill it with warm (not hot!) water. Drop in one denture or retainer cleaning tablet. Once it’s fizzing away, plop your mouthguard in there. Let it soak for about 15 to 30 minutes, or whatever the tablet’s instructions say. Don’t leave it in overnight, though, unless the box specifically says that’s cool. When time’s up, pull it out and rinse it really well under running water. Give it a good scrub with a soft brush, and boom – fresh mouthguard. Just make sure to use non-abrasive tablets; some hardcore denture cleaners might be a bit too intense for your specific mouthguard material.

DIY Solutions: Vinegar & Baking Soda for a Natural Clean

Don’t have any fancy tablets? No problem! Your kitchen probably holds the secret to a super clean mouthguard: good old white vinegar and baking soda. Yep, the same stuff you use to clean your drains and make your cookies fluffy can also rescue your mouthguard from the dark side.

First up, vinegar. That stuff isn’t just for salad dressing; it’s a bacterial assassin. Its acidity is fantastic at fighting off those little germs that cause bad smells and generally make your mouthguard feel… well, icky. To use it, mix equal parts white vinegar and water in a bowl. Dunk your mouthguard in there and let it soak for about 15 to 30 minutes.

After its vinegar bath, it’s baking soda’s turn. This stuff is a double threat: it’s a gentle scrub that helps buff away stains, and it’s a fantastic deodorizer. You know, for when your mouthguard has that “I’ve been in someone’s mouth all night” kind of smell. Just make a thick paste with baking soda and a tiny bit of water. Grab a soft toothbrush and gently scrub your mouthguard all over with the paste. This combo is a natural powerhouse for getting rid of stains on mouthguard and that bad smell mouthguard sometimes gets. The most crucial step? Rinse, rinse, rinse! And then rinse again. Seriously, unless you want your mouth to taste like a pickle dipped in a science experiment, rinse it until there’s no trace of vinegar or baking soda left. You’ve been warned!

The Ultrasonic Cleaner: High-Tech Sparkle (For the Tech-Savvy)

Alright, for those of you who like your cleaning with a side of futuristic tech, meet the ultrasonic cleaner. This isn’t some science fiction prop; it’s a real device that uses sound waves to get your mouthguard squeaky clean. Pretty neat, right?

So, how does it work? These little machines fill with water, and when you turn them on, they send out super high-frequency sound waves. These waves create zillions of tiny, microscopic bubbles that form and then immediately burst. This process, called “cavitation” (sounds fancy, is just tiny bubbles popping), creates miniature scrubbers that blast away dirt, food bits, and all that gross biofilm from every nook and cranny of your mouthguard. It’s seriously good at getting into places your toothbrush just can’t reach.

The best part? An ultrasonic cleaner is a champ at tackling even the most stubborn stains on mouthguard and absolutely demolishing that bad smell mouthguard can develop. To use it, just fill the basin with water – you can add a tiny drop of mild, non-abrasive soap if you want an extra boost, but often just water works fine. Pop your mouthguard in, close the lid, and hit the start button. Most machines run for a few minutes, maybe 5-10. Always check your specific cleaner’s instructions, though. And of course, once it’s done, pull out your mouthguard and rinse it thoroughly before putting it back in your mouth. You’ll be amazed at how fresh and clean it feels!

Cleaning Conundrums: What NOT to Do (And How to Avoid Common Mouthguard Mayhem)

Just as important as knowing what to do is knowing what to furiously avoid. These common ‘hacks’ are often myths that will damage your mouthguard and leave you with a problem (or a melted mess). Seriously, nobody wants a melted mess.

The Hot Water Hazard: Say No to Warping and Shrinkage

Ever accidentally shrunk your favorite sweater in the wash? Or left a plastic toy out in the scorching sun? Congrats, you’ve basically seen what hot water does to your mouthguard. It’s like a tiny, custom-fit disaster waiting to happen. Most mouthguards, especially the ones molded to your teeth, are made of special plastic. And plastic, as we all know, gets a little weird when things heat up.

So, here’s the deal: hot water is a definite no-go. We’re talking about boiling water, obviously. But also tossing it in the dishwasher (don’t even think about it!). Even leaving your mouthguard chilling in a hot car can be enough to mess it up. Know what I mean? The heat makes the plastic soft and bendy. And once it cools, it might not go back to its original shape.

This is called “thermal distortion,” which just means it gets all twisted and out of whack because of the heat. And that perfectly snug fit you paid for? Gone. Poof! Now you’ve got a mouthguard that either won’t stay in or feels totally wrong. It’ll be uncomfortable, awkward, and basically useless for protecting your teeth. Frustrating, right? And then you’re stuck buying a whole new one. So, remember: cold water is your mouthguard’s bestie.

Harsh Chemicals & Abrasives: Damaging Your Investment

Okay, so you want your mouthguard clean, sparkling, and germ-free. Totally get it. But before you go grabbing every strong cleaner under the sink, take a breath. Because some of those “super clean” solutions are actually super bad news for your mouthguard – and potentially for you.

Think about it: your mouthguard lives in your mouth. So, anything you put on it can end up in your mouth. Using harsh chemicals like bleach, rubbing alcohol, or those super-strong household disinfectants? That’s like asking for trouble. These things can degrade the material, meaning they literally start to break down the plastic. And who wants weird-tasting, slowly dissolving plastic in their mouth? Not to mention, these chemicals can leave toxic gunk behind that irritates your gums and other soft tissues. Ouch.

And here’s another common mistake: using regular toothpaste. I know, it cleans your teeth, so why not your mouthguard? But most toothpastes have tiny abrasive bits – little scrubbers – that are great for enamel but terrible for mouthguard plastic. They create microscopic scratches on the surface. And guess what loves to hang out in tiny scratches? Yeah, bacteria. So, you’re actually making it harder to get truly clean later. Stick to gentle soap and cold water. Your mouth and your mouthguard will thank you.

Beyond the Clean: Mouthguard Storage Secrets & When to Replace

Okay, so you’ve nailed the whole “how to clean your mouthguard” thing. You’re practically a dental hygiene wizard, swishing and scrubbing like a pro. But cleaning is just one piece of the puzzle. Now, let’s talk about keeping that bad boy in tip-top shape for the long haul. And more importantly, how to know when it’s officially time to send it to the great mouthguard graveyard in the sky.

Air It Out: The Best Way to Store Your Mouthguard

You just brushed, rinsed, and maybe even did a fancy mouthwash soak. Awesome! But if you just toss your still-damp mouthguard into a sealed case, you’re basically creating a tiny, warm, humid nightclub for bacteria. And trust me, you don’t want those little critters having a rave on something you put in your mouth every night (or during every game). Gross, right?

The secret? A clean, dry, and vented case. Seriously, those little holes aren’t just for decoration. They let air move around, drying out your mouthguard completely and stopping that gross germ party before it even starts. Think of it like airing out your gym shoes after a workout instead of sealing them in a plastic bag. Same concept, less foot odor. So, make sure your guard is totally dry before stashing it away.

And here’s a thing: your case needs cleaning too! It’s not just a fancy holder; it gets exposed to your mouthguard’s gunk every day. Give it a good scrub with soap and water every few days, or toss it in the dishwasher if it’s safe. A clean case equals a cleaner mouthguard, which equals a happier you. It’s not rocket science, just basic common sense.

Recognizing Wear & Tear: When It’s Time for a New Mouthguard

Even the best-cared-for mouthguards don’t last forever. They’re designed to take a beating, whether it’s from teeth grinding all night or a rogue elbow on the field. So, how do you know your trusty mouth pal is on its last legs?

First, look for discoloration that just won’t scrub off. If it’s gone from its original clear (or cool color) to, say, a questionable yellow or swampy green, and no amount of cleaning helps, it’s probably time. Also, check for cracks, tears, or thinning spots. Your mouthguard’s job is to protect your teeth, right? Well, a cracked or torn guard is like wearing a helmet with a big dent in it. It’s not going to do its job properly, and that means your teeth aren’t getting the protection they deserve.

And if it smells like a locker room decided to move into your mouth, that’s a HUGE red flag. A persistent, weird odor usually means bacteria have set up shop deep within the material. Also, if it feels loose or doesn’t fit snugly anymore, it’s lost its “retentive properties”—meaning it won’t stay put, which makes it useless.

Generally, most dentists suggest replacing night guards every 6-12 months and athletic guards about once a year, or if it shows any of these signs. But honestly, your dentist is the real expert here. Always check with them if you’re unsure. They’ll tell you straight if your mouthguard is still a superhero or just a tired old piece of plastic.

Quick Reality Check: Your Mouthguard Deserves Better

Alright, so we’ve had our little chat about mouthguards. And honestly? Keeping yours clean isn’t some Olympic sport or a chore from a horror movie. It’s actually super simple, and it stops a whole parade of gross problems before they even think about showing up. Think of it as a tiny, daily investment in not getting weird mouth infections.

So here’s the deal: make it a habit. A quick rinse and brush after you wear it, every single time. And then, once a week, give it a nice, relaxing deep clean bath. Seriously, that’s all it takes. No excuses, okay? It’s like brushing your teeth – you just do it.

Because ultimately, a sparkling clean mouthguard isn’t just about avoiding funky smells or looking fresh. It’s about protecting your actual health from all those sneaky bacteria. And let’s be real, it also protects your wallet by making your pricey mouthguard last way, way longer. Your mouth, and your future self, will definitely send you a thank-you note.

ü§Ø Quick Reality Check: Margins vs. Indents

Lots of people get margins and indents mixed up. They look similar, but they do totally different jobs.

Margins are the border around the whole page. They tell the text where to start and stop on every single line. The margin applies to the entire page or document.

Indents are where the text starts for a specific paragraph only. If you hit the Tab key, you’re creating an indent. You can also drag the small triangles on the ruler (those are the indents) to move the text in from the margin.

The point is, if you need to adjust the space on all four sides of your page, you need to change the margins. If you just need to move one paragraph slightly, you change the indent. Got it?

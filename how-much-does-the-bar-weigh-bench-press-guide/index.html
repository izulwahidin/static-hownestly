  • Trap Bar (or Hex Bar): This is the bar shaped like a hexagon (a six-sided shape) that you step inside. It‚Äôs mostly for deadlifts and shrugs. These are heavy, usually 45 to 65 pounds.

Some bench presses are built into a machine where the bar slides up and down on a track. This is called a Smith Machine.

  • Worse, some of them are “counter-weighted,” which means they have a hidden pulley system that makes the bar feel even lighter, almost like zero weight.
  • Hot Tip: If your gym has a Smith machine, look for a small sticker on the frame. It usually tells you the bar’s exact weight.

üî¢ The Dreaded Barbell Plate Math: Keep It Simple

So, you have your 45-pound bar. Now you need to add the plates. This is where people mess up the math and accidentally give themselves a confidence boost (or a depressing reality check).

Here’s the trick: always add up the plates, then add the bar last.

For example, let’s say you have a 45-pound bar. On each side, you put one 45-pound plate and one 10-pound plate.

  1. Plates on the left: $45 \text{ lbs} + 10 \text{ lbs} = 55 \text{ lbs}$
  2. Plates on the right: $45 \text{ lbs} + 10 \text{ lbs} = 55 \text{ lbs}$
  3. Total Plate Weight: $55 \text{ lbs} + 55 \text{ lbs} = 110 \text{ lbs}$
  4. Add the Bar: $110 \text{ lbs} + 45 \text{ lbs} = 155 \text{ lbs Total}$

See? It’s not rocket science, but you have to do the steps.
